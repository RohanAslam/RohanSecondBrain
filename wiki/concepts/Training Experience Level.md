# Training Experience Level

A classification of a lifter's training history, used to calibrate appropriate [[Training Volume]], intensity, and program complexity. The primary lens through which [[Jeff Nippard]] recommends volume in [[How many sets do you need?]].

---

## Standard Categories

| Level | Training History | Characteristics |
|---|---|---|
| Beginner | < 12 months | Responds to almost any stimulus; very high stimulus-to-fatigue ratio |
| Intermediate | 1–4 years | Requires structured progressive volume; 3–5 days/week optimal |
| Advanced | 4+ years | Needs high volume and specificity; marginal gains require more work |

---

## Practical Implication

Beginners should resist the urge to follow advanced programs — they are over-engineered for a physiology that doesn't yet need them. Conversely, advanced lifters following beginner programs will stall.
