# Training Volume

The total amount of work performed in training, typically measured as **sets per muscle group per week**. Widely considered the most important variable for driving [[Hypertrophy]] over time.

---

## Evidence-Based Ranges

Per [[How many sets do you need?]] by [[Jeff Nippard]]:

| Experience Level | Training History | Sets per Muscle / Week |
|---|---|---|
| Beginner | < 12 months | 1–5 |
| Intermediate | 1–4 years | 8–15 |
| Advanced | 4+ years | 12–20+ |

These ranges assume reasonable effort per set. Volume must be balanced against recovery capacity.

---

## Key Considerations

- **Experience level** is the primary determinant of how much volume is needed — see [[Training Experience Level]]
- **Muscle-specific variation**: back, shoulders, glutes, and quads tend to respond better to higher volumes
- **Time constraints** are a legitimate modifier — sub-optimal volume done consistently beats theoretically optimal volume done sporadically
- Higher volume must eventually be backed by [[Progressive Overload]] to drive continued adaptation
