# How Many Sets Do You Need?

**Original source:** `raw/articles/How many sets do you need?.md`

---

## Summary

The number of sets needed to build muscle is primarily determined by two factors: **training experience level** and **available time**. [[Jeff Nippard]] lays out a tiered framework based on how long someone has been lifting, with progressively higher [[Training Volume]] requirements at each stage.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Beginners (< 12 months)** can build muscle effectively with just **1–5 sets per muscle per week**, using two full-body workouts. The stimulus-to-fatigue ratio is high because everything is novel.

2. **Intermediate lifters (1–4 years)** need roughly **8–15 sets per muscle per week**, training 3–5 days weekly. The body has adapted and requires more total work to keep progressing.

3. **Advanced lifters (4+ years)** typically require **12–20+ sets per muscle per week** because adaptation is deeper and the margin for further progress is smaller.

4. **Individual muscle variation** — back, shoulders, glutes, and quads tend to respond better to higher volumes than other muscle groups.

5. **Time constraints are a legitimate factor** — more sets help, but good results don't require three hours a day. The article explicitly validates adjusting volume to fit real life.
