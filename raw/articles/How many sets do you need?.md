## Summary
- Beginners need only 1-5 sets per muscle per week and can build muscle effectively with two full-body workouts
- Intermediate lifters should progress to 8-15 sets per muscle per week, training 3-5 days weekly
- Advanced lifters typically need 12-20 sets per muscle per week for continued progress, with some muscles responding better to higher volumes

- The number of sets you need to build muscle depends on your training experience level. Beginners can make great gains with minimal volume (1-5 sets per muscle weekly), intermediates need more work (8-15 sets), and advanced lifters require higher volumes (12-20+ sets) because their bodies adapt faster. Your available time also matters—you can adjust frequency and intensity based on your schedule.

- **Training experience matters**: The article categorizes lifters as beginners (<12 months), intermediate (1-4 years), and advanced (4+ years), with each requiring progressively more volume
- **Research support**: Cites a study showing beginners can build muscle with just 1-5 sets per muscle per week
- **Individual variation**: Notes that certain muscles (back, shoulders, glutes, quads) respond better to higher volumes, and some lifters respond well to low volumes if they train hard enough
- **Practical approach**: Emphasizes balancing optimal results with time constraints—more sets help but aren't always necessary for good progress
